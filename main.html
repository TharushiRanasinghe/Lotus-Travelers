Kandy Sacred City


About :


This medieval city is surrounded by lush forests, vast mountain ranges, the meandering Kandy Lake, often known as the Sea of Milk, and a variety of notable colonial structures with stunning Kandyan-inspired architecture. Despite being only 72 miles (115 kilometers) inland, this lovely, relaxed capital has outstanding cultural and historical attractions nestled among forested hills. Due to the prominence and the spiritual significance of the Temple of the Tooth Relic (a sacred tooth of the Buddha), which is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Kandy is a sacred city. The Festival of the Tooth, also known as the Kandy Esala Perahera is a significant 10-day religious ceremony that takes place there every August.

The Knuckles and Hunnasgiriya Mountain Ranges surround Kandy, which is located at an elevation of 1640 feet (500 meters) above sea level. As a result, the area around Kandy experiences milder temperatures and more pleasant weather than the lowlands. Kandy, a significant and well-known Buddhist site, was once known as Senkadagalapura. Before the British took control of the country in 1815, it served as the last the seat of the Sinhalese kingdom.
